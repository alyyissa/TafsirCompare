## Biography

Imam Abū ʿAbd Allāh Muḥammad ibn Aḥmad ibn Abī Bakr al-Anṣārī al-Khazrajī al-Andalusī al-Qurṭubī, commonly known as al-Qurṭubī, was born in 1214 in the famous city of Córdoba in Islamic Spain (al-Andalus). His lineage traced back to the Ansar of Medina, specifically the Khazraj tribe. Despite humble beginnings, he pursued knowledge with dedication while helping support his family.

During his youth, Córdoba fell to Christian forces in 1236 under King Ferdinand III. Following this political upheaval, al-Qurṭubī migrated eastward to Egypt. He first settled in Alexandria and later moved to Cairo before eventually residing in Munīyat Banī Khusayb near modern-day Minya. There he spent the remainder of his life devoted to scholarship, worship, and teaching.

Al-Qurṭubī became widely known as a scholar of jurisprudence, theology, hadith, Arabic language, and Qur’anic interpretation. He followed the **Maliki school of jurisprudence** and the **Ashʿari theological tradition**. His personal life was marked by humility and asceticism; historians describe him as a man who lived simply while dedicating himself to learning and writing.

His most famous work is **al-Jāmiʿ li-Aḥkām al-Qurʾān**, often referred to as *Tafsīr al-Qurṭubī*. This monumental commentary spans around twenty volumes and is considered one of the greatest classical tafsirs. While its main objective is to derive legal rulings from the Qur’an, it also includes discussions of language, grammar, rhetoric, variant readings, and narrations from earlier scholars. The introduction of the book is especially famous for its detailed explanation of the sciences of the Qur’an.

Beyond tafsīr, al-Qurṭubī authored numerous other works addressing theology, remembrance of God, and matters of the afterlife. His scholarship gained respect throughout the Islamic world and influenced generations of scholars.

Al-Qurṭubī passed away on 29 April 1273 (9 Shawwāl 671 AH) in Egypt. He was buried in Munīyat Banī Khusayb, where a mosque was later constructed over his grave in recognition of his scholarly legacy.

## Exegetical Methodology

Juristic approach focusing on deriving legal rulings and insights from the Qur’an. His tafsīr combines legal analysis with linguistic explanation, rhetorical discussion, variant readings, and narrations from earlier Islamic scholars.

## Key Contributions

- Authored the monumental Qur’anic commentary **al-Jāmiʿ li-Aḥkām al-Qurʾān**
- Developed one of the most comprehensive juristic tafsirs in Islamic scholarship
- Provided detailed discussions on the sciences of the Qur’an in the introduction of his tafsīr
- Produced influential works on theology, remembrance of God, and the afterlife
