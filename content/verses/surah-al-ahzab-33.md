title: "Surah Al-Aḥzāb (الأحزاب) – Verse 33"
interpretersCount: 5
arabicVerse: |
  وَقَرْنَ فِي بُيُوتِكُنَّ وَلَا تَبَرَّجْنَ تَبَرُّجَ الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ الْأُولَىٰ ۖ وَأَقِمْنَ الصَّلَاةَ وَآتِينَ الزَّكَاةَ وَأَطِعْنَ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ ۚ إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ اللَّهُ لِيُذْهِبَ عَنكُمُ الرِّجْسَ أَهْلَ الْبَيْتِ وَيُطَهِّرَكُمْ تَطْهِيرًا
transliteration: "Wa qarna fi buyutikunna wa la tabarrajna tabarruja al-jahiliyyati al-ula... innama yuridu Allahu li-yudhiba 'ankumu ar-rijsa ahla al-bayti wa yutahhirakum tathiran"
translation: "Stay in your houses, and do not flaunt your finery like the former [days of] ignorance... Indeed Allah desires to repel all impurity from you, O People of the Household, and purify you with a thorough purification."
contextSignificance: "Verse 33:33, specifically the 'Purification Clause' (Ayat al-Tathir), is a focal point of Islamic theology. While the surrounding text addresses the Prophet's wives, this clause contains a sudden shift from feminine to masculine pronouns. This shift, along with the emphatic promise of total purification, triggers deep debates over the identity of the 'Ahl al-Bayt' and the nature of their sanctity (Ismah)."
themes:
  - "Identity of Ahl al-Bayt"
  - "Divine Purification (Tathir)"
  - "Infallibility (Ismah)"
  - "Grammatical Pronoun Shift"
  - "Event of the Cloak (Hadith al-Kisa)"
exegetes:
  - name: "Abul Aʿlā Mawdūdī"
    text: "Mawdūdī argues for a dual inclusion: the wives are the primary addressees due to context, but the Prophet (s) extended the title to Ali, Fatima, Hasan, and Husayn. He views 'purification' as a divine *intention* and an exhortation to high moral standards rather than an absolute declaration of sinlessness or a completed state of infallibility."
  - name: "Al-Qurṭubī"
    text: "Al-Qurṭubī provides a technical grammatical analysis, noting the term 'Ahl al-Bayt' is in the accusative of praise (madh). He presents various views without a single definitive conclusion, though he highlights the intensity of 'tathiran' as a verbal noun used for maximum emphasis on the removal of spiritual filth."
  - name: "Ibn Kathīr"
    text: "Ibn Kathīr maintains that the wives are the core reason for the verse's revelation. However, he acknowledges the 'Event of the Cloak' traditions as authentic and widespread. He concludes that while the context applies to the wives, the Prophet’s own actions expanded the scope to include his closest blood relatives, who are also forbidden from receiving charity."
  - name: "ʿAllāmah Ṭabāṭabāʾī"
    text: "Ṭabāṭabāʾī asserts that the pronoun shift (from 'kunna' to 'kum') definitively proves a change in audience. He argues 'Purification' here is a 'creational will' (Iradah Takwiniyya)—a divine act that effectively renders the Ahl al-Bayt (specifically the Five under the Cloak) infallible. He rejects the inclusion of the wives based on the grammatical evidence and the specific restrictions in the Hadith al-Kisa."
  - name: "Al-Ṭabarsī"
    text: "Al-Ṭabarsī focuses on the particle 'Innama' (Indeed only), which he argues restricts this unique purification to a specific group. He provides extensive documentation of Sunni and Shi'i chains for the Tradition of the Cloak, concluding that this verse establishes a unique ontological status of purity and leadership for the Prophet, Ali, Fatima, Hasan, and Husayn."
criticalAnalysis: "The interpretative tension centers on whether 'Context' (the wives) or 'Tradition/Grammar' (the Five of the Cloak) takes precedence. Mawdūdī and Ibn Kathīr attempt to harmonize the two, though this often softens the emphatic language of the verse. Ṭabāṭabāʾī and Al-Ṭabarsī argue that the sudden grammatical shift and the absolute nature of 'tathiran' suggest a parenthetical declaration of a higher, infallible status for a restricted group, distinct from the conditional commands given to the wives."
