---
slug: "surah-al-baqarah-143"
title: "Surah Al-Baqarah (البقرة) – Verse 143"
interpretersCount: 4
arabicVerse: |
  وَكَذَلِكَ جَعَلْنَاكُمْ أُمَّةً وَسَطًا لِّتَكُونُواْ شُهَدَاء عَلَى النَّاسِ وَيَكُونَ الرَّسُولُ عَلَيْكُمْ شَهِيدًا...
transliteration: "Wa kadhālika jaʿalnākum ummatan wasaṭan litakūnū shuhadāʾa ʿalā an-nāsi wa yakūna ar-rasūlu ʿalaykum shahīdā..."
translation: "Thus We made you a middle nation that you may be witnesses to the people and that the Apostle may be a witness to you..."
contextSignificance: "Revealed alongside the change of the Qiblah (direction of prayer), this verse defines the identity and global mission of the Muslim community. It introduces the concept of the 'Ummatan Wasaṭan' (Middle/Balanced Nation), positioning Muslims as a mediating force between the Prophet and the rest of humanity. The verse explores the heavy responsibility of 'witnessing'—testifying to divine truth both in this world and on the Day of Judgment."
themes:
  - "The Middle/Balanced Nation (Ummatan Wasaṭan)"
  - "The Change of the Qiblah"
  - "Witnessing (Shahādah) over Humanity"
  - "Prophetic Witness over the Believers"
  - "Justice and Moderation"
exegetes:
  - name: "ʿAllāmah Ṭabāṭabāʾī"
    text: "Ṭabāṭabāʾī argues that 'witnessing' is not just a metaphor for moderation, but an 'ontological vision' granted to the purified friends of God (Awliyāʾ). He contends that because most people cannot perceive the hidden realities of deeds, the 'witnesses' mentioned here must be the Imams, who observe the spiritual states of people in this life to testify on the Day of Judgment."
  - name: "Ibn Kathīr"
    text: "Ibn Kathīr interprets 'Wasaṭ' as meaning the best, most just, and most honored. He focuses on the eschatological role of the Ummah: on the Day of Resurrection, Muslims will testify that previous Prophets (like Noah) successfully delivered their messages, even when their own people deny it. He sees this as a communal honor bestowed upon the followers of Muhammad (s)."
  - name: "Al-Qurṭubī"
    text: "Al-Qurṭubī provides a linguistic analysis, showing that 'middle' (Wasaṭ) signifies the choice part of something (like the center of a valley). He emphasizes moderation in creed—avoiding the extremes of materialism and over-spiritualization. He notes that the Prophet's testimony validates the Ummah's message, placing a burden of uprightness on every Muslim."
  - name: "Al-Ṭabarsī"
    text: "Al-Ṭabarsī highlights the hierarchy of testimony: God as the ultimate witness, the Prophet over the Muslims, and the Imams over humanity. He stresses that the earth is never devoid of these 'just witnesses.' He also notes that the change of Qiblah was a 'hard test' designed to distinguish true followers of the Messenger from those who cling to old habits."
criticalAnalysis: "The interpretative divide is between communal and selective witnesshood. Sunni exegetes (Ibn Kathīr, Al-Qurṭubī) see the 'Middle Nation' as a collective title for the entire Muslim community, emphasizing their role in history and the afterlife. In contrast, Shīʿī exegetes (Ṭabāṭabāʾī, Al-Ṭabarsī) argue that 'witnessing' requires a level of spiritual perception and infallibility that is only possible for the Prophet and the Imams, thereby protecting the verse from the contradiction of having sinful individuals act as divine witnesses."
