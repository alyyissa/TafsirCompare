---
slug: "surah-al-isra-71"
title: "Surah Al-Isra (الإسراء) – Verse 71"
interpretersCount: 4
arabicVerse: |
  يَوْمَ نَدْعُو كُلَّ أُنَاسٍ بِإِمَامِهِمْ ۖ فَمَنْ أُوتِيَ كِتَابَهُ بِيَمِينِهِ فَأُولَٰئِكَ يَقْرَءُونَ كِتَابَهُمْ وَلَا يُظْلَمُونَ فَتِيلًا
transliteration: "Yawma nad'u kulla unasin bi-imamihim fa-man utiya kitabahu bi-yaminihi fa-ula'ika yaqra'una kitabahum wa la yuzlamuna fatilan"
translation: "The day We shall call every group of people with their Imam. So whoever is given his book in his right hand—they will read their book, and they will not be wronged [so much as] a single date-thread."
contextSignificance: "This verse describes a central scene of the Day of Resurrection: the collective summoning of humanity. It establishes that judgment is not merely individual but tied to leadership and communal allegiance. The 'Imam' serves as the standard-bearer for each group, determining their trajectory toward success (the Right Hand) or failure."
themes:
  - "The Day of Resurrection (Al-Qiyamah)"
  - "The Concept of the Imam (Leader/Guide)"
  - "Accountability via the Book of Deeds"
  - "Absolute Divine Justice (The 'Fatīl')"
exegetes:
  - name: "Ibn Kathīr"
    text: "Ibn Kathīr acknowledges that 'Imam' could mean a Prophet or a Scripture, but he strongly favors the interpretation that it refers to the 'Record of Deeds.' He argues that the immediate mention of 'receiving the book' in the next sentence confirms that the Imam is the ledger itself. He highlights that being called with one's record ensures a precise, indisputable accounting of life."
  - name: "ʿAllāmah Ṭabāṭabāʾī"
    text: "Ṭabāṭabāʾī rejects the 'Record of Deeds' interpretation, noting that a record is a 'follower' of actions, not an 'Imam' (leader). He insists the verse refers to actual leaders—whether Prophets and divinely appointed guides or tyrants and corrupters. Your destiny is inextricably linked to the 'Wilayah' (authority) you accepted in the world."
  - name: "Al-Qurṭubī"
    text: "Al-Qurṭubī provides a characteristic encyclopedia of views, including the 'Imam of the Age' and even the eccentric view that people are called by their mothers. He ultimately aligns with the 'Record of Deeds' or 'Scripture' view as the most textually grounded, emphasizing that the 'Fatīl' (the tiny thread in a date pit) proves no good deed is too small to be rewarded."
  - name: "Al-Ṭabarsī"
    text: "Al-Ṭabarsī centers the verse on the Shīʿī doctrine of the Infallible Imams. He cites traditions stating that 'we [the Ahl al-Bayt] are their Imams.' He explains that on Judgment Day, the cry will go out: 'Where are the followers of [Prophet/Imam]?' This identifies the 'Imam' as the ultimate criterion for salvation or perdition."
criticalAnalysis: "The primary exegetical divide is between a 'Documentary' view (Imam = Book/Record) and a 'Leadership' view (Imam = Guide/Prophet). Sunni commentators like Ibn Kathīr tend toward the former to emphasize individual accountability through text. Shīʿī commentators like Ṭabāṭabāʾī and Al-Ṭabarsī argue the latter is linguistically superior, as an 'Imam' must be something that is followed. This second view adds a layer of political and spiritual responsibility: choosing the right leadership in this life is the prerequisite for receiving the Book in the right hand in the next."
