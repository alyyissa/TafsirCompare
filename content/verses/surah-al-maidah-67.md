---
slug: surah-al-maidah-67
title: Surah Al-Ma’idah (المائدة) – Verse 67
interpretersCount: 4
arabicVerse: يَا أَيُّهَا الرَّسُولُ بَلِّغْ مَا أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ مِن رَّبِّكَ ۖ وَإِن لَّمْ تَفْعَلْ فَمَا بَلَّغْتَ رِسَالَتَهُ ۚ وَاللَّهُ يَعْصِمُكَ مِنَ النَّاسِ...
transliteration: "Ya ayyuha ar-rasulu balligh ma unzila ilayka min rabbika wa in lam taf'al fama ballaghta risalatahu wa Allahu ya'simuka min an-nasi..."
translation: "O Apostle! Communicate that which has been sent down to you from your Lord, and if you do not, you will not have communicated His message. Allah shall protect you from the people."
contextSignificance: "Known as the 'Verse of Tabligh' (Proclamation), this is one of the most structurally unique verses in the Quran. It presents an ultimatum: the entire 23-year mission of the Prophet rests upon the delivery of this one specific message. It also contains a rare, explicit guarantee of divine protection ('Ya’simuka'), suggesting the Prophet anticipated severe backlash or accusations of personal bias upon its announcement."
themes:
  - "The Ultimate Proclamation"
  - "Divine Protection (Ismah) from People"
  - "Completeness of the Prophetic Mission"
  - "The Appointment of Succession"
exegetes:
  - name: "Ibn Kathīr"
    text: "Ibn Kathīr interprets this as a general reinforcement of the Prophet's duty to convey *all* revelation. He views the 'protection' as physical safety, noting that after this verse, the Prophet dismissed his bodyguards. For him, the verse is a testament to the Prophet’s courage and the fact that he withheld absolutely nothing from the people."
  - name: "ʿAllāmah Ṭabāṭabāʾī"
    text: "Ṭabāṭabāʾī argues that the message must be a specific, 'new' directive rather than the religion as a whole. He identifies it as the appointment of Ali (as) at Ghadir Khumm. He reasons that the Prophet's hesitation wasn't personal fear, but concern that people would mistake the appointment for nepotism, potentially causing a schism."
  - name: "Al-Qurṭubī"
    text: "Al-Qurṭubī focuses on the ethical duty of scholars and leaders not to hide the truth. He treats the verse as a refutation of any claim that secret parts of the Quran exist. He emphasizes the 'Divine Guarantee,' arguing that once Allah promised protection, no human conspiracy could derail the completion of Islam."
  - name: "Al-Ṭabarsī"
    text: "Al-Ṭabarsī links the verse directly to the historical event of Ghadir Khumm. He cites numerous Sunni and Shīʿī sources to show that the Prophet received this verse while traveling back from the Farewell Pilgrimage. He argues that the 'success' of the mission was tied to establishing a clear line of divinely-guided leadership (Imamah)."
criticalAnalysis: "The primary point of contention is the 'Referent': what was the specific message? Sunni commentators (Ibn Kathir, Al-Qurtubi) suggest it refers to the entirety of Islam or the People of the Book, yet struggle to explain why this specific command is framed as an all-or-nothing ultimatum. Shīʿī commentators (Tabataba'i, Al-Tabarsi) argue that only the appointment of a successor (Wilayah) fits the gravity of the language, as it provides the 'Guardian' necessary to keep the religion intact after the Prophet’s passing."
