slug: "surah-al-shura-23"
title: "Surah Al-Shūrā (الشورى) – Verse 23"
interpretersCount: 6
arabicVerse: |
  ذَلِكَ الَّذِي يُبَشِّرُ اللَّهُ عِبَادَهُ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ قُل لّا أَسْأَلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ أَجْرًا إِلاَّ الْمَوَدَّةَ فِي الْقُرْبَى...
transliteration: "Qul lā as’alukum ʿalayhi ajran illā al-mawaddata fī al-qurbā..."
translation: "Say, ‘I do not ask you any reward for it except love of [my] relatives.’"
contextSignificance: "Commonly known as the 'Verse of Mawaddah' (Affection), this text addresses the Prophet's sincerity. While most prophets in the Quran declare they ask for 'no reward' (no wage), this verse introduces a 'Connected Exception.' The central debate is whether this 'love' is a tribal appeal for safety, a metaphorical call to seek nearness to God, or a divinely mandated devotion to the Prophet’s Household (Ahl al-Bayt) as the guardians of the message."
themes:
  - "The Prophet’s Wage (Ajr)"
  - "Mawaddah (Devotional Love)"
  - "The Status of Al-Qurbā"
  - "Sincerity vs. Accusations of Personal Gain"
exegetes:
  - name: "Al-Qurṭubī"
    text: "Al-Qurṭubī leans toward a 'tribal safety' reading, where the Prophet asks the Quraysh to at least honor their blood ties to him and stop their persecution. However, he inclusively records the traditions identifying 'Al-Qurbā' as Ali, Fatimah, and their sons, acknowledging their high rank in Islamic piety."
  - name: "Ibn Kathīr"
    text: "Ibn Kathīr views the request as directed at the Quraysh: if they won't follow the message, they should at least respect kinship bonds. He emphasizes that the Prophet isn't seeking money or power, but simply the freedom to preach without being harmed by his own relatives."
  - name: "Abul Aʿlā Mawdūdī"
    text: "Mawdūdī strongly rejects the 'Prophet's Family' interpretation as logically inconsistent with a Meccan setting. He prefers interpreting 'Qurbā' as 'nearness to God,' meaning the Prophet's only reward is for the people to reform themselves and draw closer to Allah."
  - name: "Al-Ṭabarsī"
    text: "Al-Ṭabarsī asserts that 'Al-Qurbā' refers exclusively to the Ahl al-Bayt (as). He argues this 'reward' actually benefits the believers, as loving the Prophet's family ensures the community remains attached to authentic, divinely-purified leadership after his death."
  - name: "Al-Rāzī"
    text: "Al-Rāzī highlights the spiritual necessity of this love. He cites famous traditions stating that dying with love for the family of Muhammad (saw) is the mark of a martyr and a perfected believer. He famously reconciles this by comparing the Ahl al-Bayt to Noah's Ark and the Companions to guiding stars."
  - name: "ʿAllāmah Ṭabāṭabāʾī"
    text: "Ṭabāṭabāʾī argues that 'Mawaddah' is a 'Connected Exception.' The love is part of the message itself; by loving the Household, the community refers back to them for religious knowledge. He dismisses other views as linguistically weak or historically implausible (e.g., the Ansar already loved him)."
criticalAnalysis: "The primary tension is between 'Tribal Civility' and 'Sacred Devotion.' If the verse is a plea for safety, it is a temporary historical request. If it refers to 'nearness to God,' it becomes an abstract metaphor. However, if it refers to the Prophet’s Household, it becomes a structural pillar of the faith—a 'wage' that is paid back to the community in the form of preserved guidance. The use of the word 'Mawaddah' (intense, active affection) suggests a bond far deeper than simple tribal non-aggression."
